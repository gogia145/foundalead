Home

Our goal is to build Leaders with high ethics and integrity. Many leaders lord it over others to serve themselves while ethical leaders lord it over themselves to serve others. The difference is largely between selfishness and selflessness. An ethical leader goes in the front, leads the way and by his examples and influence , builds voluntary followership and alignment. No coercion. No pressure . Good leaders are imaginative and creative at handling people and situations, they anticipate problems and look for ways to mitigate problems well in advance. They are totally humble and very deferential with little worries over who gets credit; as a result, the power they wield really never gets into their heads. Leaders develop their people by setting realistic and achievable goals and expectations. True leaders have a mental toughness that is in tandem with their physical stamina; no one ever leads with a sick mind and body.
